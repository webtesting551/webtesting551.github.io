Machiavelli's Sinister Objective for Prospective Princes

March 27, 2021

At face value, Niccolò Machiavelli’s The Prince appears to be a gift for successful and lasting rule. However, similar to the underlying deceit in centaur Nessus’ gift to Deianeira that fatally poisoned Hercules, there is buried a sinister objective of actually leading new and prospective princes to their downfall. This is cunningly achieved by deeply embedding flawed advice and camouflaging it amongst sound rules. To identify these pitfalls, the ruling of states and the recommended character of a prince are explored within this paper.
