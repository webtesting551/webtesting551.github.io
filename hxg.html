Heat Exchanger Design for Oil Cooling in a Gasoline IC Engine using ANSYS Fluent

April 3, 2023

Documentation

Download Report

Objective

To design a heat exchanger that is capable of cooling down hot motor oil at 350°F in a gasoline internal combustion engine under specified maximum load conditions. The design's aim is to reduce the oil temperature to 215°F, while maintaining realistic dimensions.

Software Used

Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) software, Ansys Fluent, was employed for transient thermal analysis, encompassing simulations and meshing of the duct and heat exchanger models.

Skills Learned

  • Thermal Analysis: Implementation of transient thermal analysis in heat exchanger design.
  • Materials Selection: Selection of materials such as aluminum, copper, and polypropylene, based on material properties.
  • Simulation and Modeling: Utilization of Ansys Fluent for heat exchanger and duct model simulations and meshing.
  • Mathematical Calculations: Employing mathematical formulations including the Darcy-Weisbach equation, Energy Balance Equation, and Colebrook equation for heat transfer and pressure drop computations.

Design and Assumptions

  • Initial Design Statement: Compact heat exchanger engineering to achieve specific temperature reduction in a gasoline IC engine.
  • Materials Selection: Utilization of aluminum (shell), copper (oil piping), and polypropylene (air duct).
  • Design Features: Emphasis on surface area-to-volume ratio, realistic dimensions, and temperature, flow rate, and dimensional constraints.
  • Simulation and Analysis: Application of Ansys Fluent for comprehensive simulations, meshing, and analysis.

Method

The methodology followed in this project can be summarized in the following stages:

  1. Requirement Analysis: Identification and definition of the temperature reduction requirements, constraints, and specifications for the heat exchanger.
  2. Material Selection and Design: Selection of appropriate materials (aluminum, copper, polypropylene) based on their thermal properties, followed by initial design conceptualization, considering factors like high surface area-to-volume ratio and realistic dimensions.
  3. Mathematical Modeling: Application of relevant equations such as the Darcy-Weisbach equation, Energy Balance Equation, and Colebrook equation for precise heat transfer calculations, pressure drop analysis, and efficiency estimations.
  4. Simulation and Analysis: Utilization of Ansys Fluent for transient thermal analysis, including meshing, simulation, and evaluation of the duct and heat exchanger models.
  5. Optimization and Iterative Design: Iterative design improvements and optimization of the model to meet the desired efficiency and performance criteria.
  6. Validation and Testing: Experimental validation and testing of the design, using standardized procedures to ensure that the design meets the specified objectives.
  7. Documentation and Reporting: Comprehensive documentation of the entire design process, methodologies employed, simulation results, and final conclusions, ensuring adherence to relevant industry standards.

Limitations

  • Assumptions in Design: Potential inaccuracies stemming from temperature, flow rate, and dimensional assumptions.
  • Single-Phase Fluids: ε-NTU method's restriction to single-phase fluids in efficiency calculations.
  • Software Constraints: Limitations pertaining to meshing and simulations, such as tetra-collapse avoidance.

Project Results

  • Heat Exchanger Design: Consideration of two cylindrical design iterations.
  • Simulations: Extensive simulations for temperature, pressure, velocity analysis.
  • Efficiency: 89.1% efficiency achieved through the ε-NTU method.
  • Conclusion: Successful design execution in lowering oil temperature, satisfying criteria, and providing a feasible motor oil cooling solution.
